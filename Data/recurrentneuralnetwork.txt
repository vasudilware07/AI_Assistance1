Recurrent neural networks are a type of artificial neural network that are particularly well-suited to processing sequential data, such as time series data, natural language text, or speech. Unlike traditional feedforward neural networks, RNNs have connections that allow information to flow from one time step to the next, allowing them to maintain a kind of "memory" of previous inputs.

There are several different types of RNNs, including simple RNNs, long short-term memory (LSTM) networks, and gated recurrent units (GRUs). Each of these types of RNNs has its own strengths and weaknesses, and is suited to different types of tasks.

RNNs have been used to solve a wide variety of problems, including language modeling, machine translation, speech recognition, and time series forecasting. They have also been used to generate music, art, and even poetry!
